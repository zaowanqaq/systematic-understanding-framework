# Systematic Understanding Framework

> A multi-dimensional framework for building deep, structured understanding of any complex topic — based on the **"How to Understand Anything"** methodology by Mauricio Heck (Polymath Investor).

## What is this?

When faced with a complex system, a new domain, or a challenging concept, most people either drown in details or settle for superficial knowledge. This framework gives you a structured, repeatable process to build genuine depth.

It organizes **22 thinking tools** across four progressive stages:

| Stage | Focus | Core Question |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| **1. Deconstruct & Anchor** | Break complexity down to fundamentals | "What is absolutely certain here?" |
| **2. Connect & Patternize** | Link new ideas to what you already know | "What does this remind me of?" |
| **3. Question & Reframe** | Challenge assumptions and find better problems | "What if the opposite is true?" |
| **4. Integrate & Verify** | Test understanding through output and feedback | "Can I explain this to a complete beginner?" |

## Tools Included

- **First Principles Thinking** — rebuild from undeniable truths
- **Decomposition** — divide and conquer complexity
- **Pareto Lock-In** — find the 20% that drives 80%
- **Analogical Mapping** — connect new to known
- **Inversion** — solve by avoiding failure
- **Five Whys** — drill to root cause
- **Socratic Questioning** — interrogate your own beliefs
- **Feynman Technique** — explain it like you're teaching a beginner
- **Cross-domain Integration** — build a latticework of mental models
- ...and 13 more

## Why "Systematic Understanding"?

Most "thinking frameworks" are collections of isolated tips. This one is **systematic** — it guides you through a complete arc from first encounter to verified mastery, with each stage building on the last.
